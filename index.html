PianoPrints

by original composer & performer Gregory Wisener.

Welcome!

PianoPrints by Gregory Wisener is an experience of romantic and relaxing original piano compositions. Perfect for any age, Wisener's compositions can primarily be found at markets in the North County area of Southern California.

From the Composer:

Why create and share music? Since the inception of human consciousness, people have been compelled to express themselves through sound. At first, we were limited to simple hand held instruments such as animal skin drums, brass and copper bells, wooden flutes- and then with the advent of civilizations, we see string and metal acoustic sound boards of the guitar, violin, and piano. Yet, no matter the technical complexity and knowledge of instrument and performer, the experience of hearing, seeing, and feeling musical expression remains much the same as it has always been- a phenomenon of the unconscious. Music lets our heart sing, our spirit soar, and our soul rejoice, celebrating our two-fold identity as unique individuals yet bound to a common field of human understanding and compassion. Hopefully, it is from this ancient tradition and spirit of music that yet another encompassing sound is made to make sense of this beautiful, yet sometimes frightening human experience. My earnest ambition is to be at once rooted in this original human artistic intention while gathering energy and momentum from a place- all of us agree is equally everyone’s home: the Future. I believe this is the sacred role all artists must consider being a part of at one time or another. In this sense, this recording is much more like a photograph than a finished product to be “consumed”- a message in a bottle from the past whose contents contain hopes and dreams of the future, much like a photograph of a person when they were a child reflects the person one does in fact come to be. All of this music is a personal reflection of living human experience and I hope it will prove to be as expressly simple, yet as subtly timeless as all the world around us, be it dandelion, redwood, person, or planet. My deepest desire and sincere wish for you is that this music be like a waterfall in the desert- certainly a pause for reflection in one’s journey and perhaps even providing a much needed artistic and human acoustic metaphor.
